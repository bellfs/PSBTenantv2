- 52 Old Elvet is the flagship PSB52 asset: 12 premium one- and two-bedroom apartments in central Durham.
- The long-term academic year model is the stable valuation-grade income base. Short-term lets are mainly July and August, with occasional September or graduation overlap when operationally sensible.

## Lettings Rules

- 52OE often has international student enquiries with slower decision cycles from January to September. Offer virtual tours, high-quality media and clear contracts.
- Do not stop viewings until a lease is signed, deposit terms are met and the property is genuinely secure.
- Same-day or first-three-hour follow-up after a viewing matters. WhatsApp follow-up is often more effective than email.
- In-person signing at the office or going to the students with the contract can materially improve conversion.
- For international transfers, it can be acceptable to sign now and allow several working days for deposit transfer if the commercial risk is understood.
- Guarantors or RentGuarantor-style alternatives should be considered where income/credit risk exists.

## Short-Let and OTA Rules

- Airbnb, Booking.com and Expedia style messages are usually related to 52 Old Elvet, 2 St Margarets Mews, 35 St Andrews or 7 Cathedrals. Cross-reference property/calendar before making assumptions.
- 52OE should ideally be represented on Booking.com as one property/multi-listing with room/apartment types, not 12 fragmented listings, to concentrate reviews and make inventory management sane.
- Never create or solicit fake reviews. Use compliant post-stay review requests only.
- Short-let operations require calendar sync, cleaner rota, linen inventory, access/code management, guest macros and review follow-up.

## Compliance and Contract Memory

- AST sample mapping notes: deposits should be protected and prescribed information served within 30 days.
- Bills-inclusive scope in 52OE ASTs can include electricity, gas, water, internet, phone, TV licence and council tax wording. Agents must check exact current contract wording before replying.
- Fair usage policy and smart-meter monitoring are essential for bills-inclusive margin protection.
- Access/quiet enjoyment: 24h notice for inspections/repairs unless emergency or contract permits otherwise.
- Contract date validation is important: manual flagged an invalid sample end date of 31/06/2026; standard June end should be 30 June.
- Tenant bedding is generally tenant responsibility unless specifically agreed. Bedding found in a flat does not automatically mean the business agreed to provide it.
- Free cleans should only be honoured when prior email/contract evidence supports them. Tunstall likely had fortnightly light cleans agreed according to Hannah/Claude inbox checking.
